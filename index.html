My Response

Image from Pixabay

For the Prompt 1, I was immediately reminded of one specific programmed pattern in nature. It is a honeycomb. Honeycomb consists of lots of cells. The cells are not placed straight in facing columns even though they are well aligned in rows; it shows they are intended to be misaligned with a certain distance (half of the cell to be exact). I see it as programmed order to fill the empty gap among the cells and increase the durability of the honeycomb. Moreover, to give a closer look, it shows that each cell is a hexagon. Hexagon looks complicated in the view of humans, but nature prefers the form and it is well known to be the shape of a honeycomb. Why does it have to be hexagon? The reason is that hexagon is the most sturdy shape that can support the weight of honey plus the power of gravity when holding as much honey as possible. One scientific fact that many would not be aware of is that the initial shape bees make is actually a circle, and then the cells change into hexagons. I don't know do bees intended it, but I found sharing the idea of "keeping a trace of your decisions" of the Prompt 1 to make the ultimate shape for better design.
